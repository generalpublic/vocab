## 2026-03-29 — Academic Writing (6 samples, batch analysis)
**Mode:** Academic/theological
**Format:** Seminary papers and discussion posts
**Word count:** ~3,900 total across 6 pieces
**Top 3 findings:**
1. Claim → Scripture → Scholar → Synthesis pattern is highly consistent — PRESERVE
2. Tricolon closings are genuinely strong ("both a creation ordinance and a gospel metaphor...") — PRESERVE
3. Uniform sentence length (25-40 words, rarely under 10) limits emphasis — REFINE (Peterson: mix lengths)
**New patterns:** Roadmap openings ("This document will outline..."), pastoral direct address ("we must," "let us"), anticipating counterarguments naturally
**Vocab words found naturally:** ordained, sanctity, imbued, imperative, commensurate

## 2026-03-29 — Professional Writing (3 samples, batch analysis)
**Mode:** Professional/corporate
**Format:** Annual self-evaluations (2021-2023)
**Word count:** ~8,500 total across 3 evals
**Top 3 findings:**
1. Quantified evidence and testimonial insertion are effective persuasion tools — PRESERVE
2. Wall-of-text paragraphs (200-400+ words) with no breaks — ROUGH (Peterson: one point per paragraph)
3. "Facilitate" used as catch-all verb obscuring what he actually did — ROUGH (Peterson: precise word choice)
**New patterns:** Evolution from hesitant 2021 to strategic promotion case in 2023. Third-person shift in 2023 is deliberate but awkward. "Above and beyond" and "pertinent" are crutch words.
**Vocab words found naturally:** none from vocab_db.json in professional writing

## 2026-03-29 — Journal Writing (7 entries, initial batch via Notion)
**Mode:** Journal/devotional
**Format:** Notion pages, stream of consciousness
**Word count:** ~5,500 total across 7 entries (2025-2026)
**Top 3 findings:**
1. Sentence rhythm variation (5-word punches → 70-word runs) is his most authentic pattern — PRESERVE
2. Register code-switching (theological + "lol" in same paragraph) is a genuine voice signature — PRESERVE
3. "Anyhow" doing too much work as a transition; throat-clearing opening sentences — REFINE
**New patterns:** Self-interrogation as thinking tool, analogies from lived experience (cancer analogy), repetition for conviction ("I will not be deceived" x3), Fight List shows natural argument structure under stakes, humor always parenthetical/self-deprecating and never undermines seriousness
**Vocab words found naturally:** cognizant, assuaging, voracious, transgress, complacent, frivolously, squander

## 2026-03-30 — COMPREHENSIVE JOURNAL ANALYSIS (150+ entries, full corpus)
**Mode:** Journal/devotional
**Format:** OneNote backups (2011-2019) + Notion databases (2019-2026)
**Word count:** ~150,000 total across 150+ entries spanning 15 years
**Sources analyzed:**
- OneNote Personal/Journal: 2011 (~17K words), 2013 (~1K), 2014 (~3K), 2015 (~2K)
- OneNote SekNotes/Journal/Journals: ~40K words (includes duplicates with 2011)
- Notion 2018-2021 Thoughts Journal: 25 entries
- Notion 2022 Thoughts Journal: 25 entries
- Notion 2023 Thoughts Journal: 7 entries
- Notion 2024 Thoughts Journal: 6 entries
- Notion Day One Archive: 25 daily diary entries (2019)
- Notion 2025 Glory Journal: 43 entries (~42K words)
- Notion 2026 Journal: 5 entries (~5K words)

**Top findings:**
1. Long-run-to-short-punch sentence rhythm is THE signature — confirmed across every year 2011-2026. Average sentence length evolved: ~45 words (2011) → ~25 (2014) → ~18 with fragments (2019+). PRESERVE.
2. "lol" is not humor — it is a structural pressure-release valve deployed after the most vulnerable statements. Load-bearing across 15 years. PRESERVE.
3. Four sub-registers within journal mode: raw confessional, declarative spiritual authority, theological reasoning, intimate tenderness. Register-shifting within single entries IS the voice. PRESERVE.
4. Concrete/irreverent analogies are his strongest rhetorical tool: Jesus reading a magazine, boiled eggs, kneeling in $1,600 pants, nail gun on the cross, Chick-fil-A as proof of planning. PRESERVE.
5. Crutch words confirmed across full corpus: "just" (extremely high frequency), "started to/began to," "truly," "things," "basically," "literally," "anyhow." REFINE.
6. 2023 is his literary peak: "forever paying interest on a principle I never deserved in the first place." The mother entries are the most powerful writing in the entire corpus. PRESERVE this register as ceiling.
7. 2024 Storytelling entry is his weakest writing — formal, generic, thesis-driven. Sounds like someone else. ROUGH — avoid this register.
8. Block paragraph habit persists across all 15 years. ROUGH.
9. He never uses em dashes, sarcasm, irony aimed at others, passive voice, literary allusion outside Scripture, scene-setting/physical description, or extended metaphor past 1-2 sentences. These absences DEFINE his voice — PRESERVE the absences.
10. Calling Jesus "cute" and "everything's rigged" are distinctively his theological language. PRESERVE.

**Trajectory arc:** Abstract theological reasoning (2011) → Embodied psychological self-knowledge (2019) → Literary compression (2023) → Distilled conviction (2025). The 2011 energy and the 2023 craft combine in the strongest 2025 passages.

**Strongest passages identified:**
1. "The Waiting Room" — Jesus reading a magazine (2025)
2. "for my rags" — fragment cascade (2025)
3. Mother's love — "forever paying interest on a principle" (2023)
4. Parents entry — parallel structure about mother's labor (2014)
5. "the cost of faith is being vulnerable to the possibility of being disappointed" (2025)
6. Boiled egg analogy — impatience/perfectionism (2014)
7. $1,600 Acronym jacket on the beach (2025)
8. "2021 was me rowing through a lake full of poop" (2022)
9. "I wanted to sin lmao" — register whiplash (2025)
10. "Am I allowed to say money doesn't buy happiness as a broke man?" (2025)

**Vocab words found naturally (full corpus):** cognizant, assuage/assuaging, voracious, transgress, complacent, frivolously, squander, ordained, sanctity, imbued, imperative, commensurate, abate, banality/banal, incessant, dissipated, calloused
